I am a final-year computer science Ph.D. candidate at <a href="https://www.utoronto.ca">University of Toronto</a> in the [Natural Language Processing Group](http://www.cs.toronto.edu/compling/index.html) working with [Yang Xu](http://www.cs.toronto.edu/~yangxu/index.html). I am broadly interested in natural language processing, machine learning, and computational linguistics. 


My current research is focused on investigating the mechanistic principles of neural language models in solving real-world problems, and how such interpretability research could help align state-of-the-art generative AI systems with human values. In particular, I am deeply interested in understanding why language models sometimes generate harmful or untruthful contents. This line of research has the potential to enhance the safety and transparency of large language models (LLMs).  

During my PhD, I studied computational modeling of creative language use such as metaphor and idiom.

I am a computer science Ph.D. candidate at <a href="https://www.utoronto.ca">University of Toronto</a> working with [Yang Xu](http://www.cs.toronto.edu/~yangxu/index.html) in the [Computational Linguistics Group](http://www.cs.toronto.edu/compling/index.html). I am broadly interested in natural language processing, machine learning, and computational linguistics. 


My current research is focused on understanding the mechanistic principles of neural networks (particularly transformer-based language models) in solving real-world problems (such as open-domain question answering) and in generating undesired outputs (such as hallucinations). I also work on aligning neural text generation systems with human values via reinforcement learning to enhance their safety and robustness. During my PhD, I studied computational modeling of creative language uses such as metaphor and idiom.

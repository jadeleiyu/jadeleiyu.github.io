I am a final-year computer science Ph.D. candidate at <a href="https://www.utoronto.ca">University of Toronto</a> in the [Natural Language Processing Group](http://www.cs.toronto.edu/compling/index.html) and a research scientist intern at [Meta FAIR](https://ai.meta.com/research/). I am broadly interested in natural language processing, machine learning, and computational linguistics. 


My current research is focused on investigating the mechanistic principles of neural language models, and how such interpretability research could help align state-of-the-art generative AI systems with human values. In particular, I am interested in understanding why language models sometimes generate harmful or untruthful outputs, and this line of research has the potential to enhance the safety and transparency of large language models (LLMs).  

During my PhD, I studied computational modeling of creative language use such as metaphor and idiom.

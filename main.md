I am a Ph.D. computer science student at <a href="https://www.utoronto.ca">University of Toronto</a> working with [Yang Xu](http://www.cs.toronto.edu/~yangxu/index.html) in the [Computational Linguistics Group](http://www.cs.toronto.edu/compling/index.html). I am broadly interested in natural language processing, machine learning, and computational linguistics. 


My current research is focused on understanding the internal mechanistic principles of neural networks (particularly language models) in solving complex real-world problems (such as mathematical reasoning) and in producing undesired generation outputs (such as hallucinations). I also work on aligning large language models with human values via reinforcement learning and self-supervised learning. During my PhD, I studied computational modeling of creative language uses such as metaphors and idioms.
